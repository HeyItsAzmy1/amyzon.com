Apple Pie

  • Time: 3 hr 30 min
  • Serves: 6-8
Ingredients Quantity
All-Purpose Flour 2 and 1/2 cups
Sugar 4 teaspoons
Salt 1/4 teaspoons
Cold Butter, diced 14 tablespoons
Egg 1
Cold Water 2 tablespoons
Lemon Juice 2 tablespoons
Baking Apples 3 pounds
Sugar 2/3 cup
Unsalted Butter 1/4 cup
Ground Cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon
Nutmeg Generous pinch
Egg, lightly beaten 1

Step 1: Make the dough by hand. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Using your fingers, work the butter into the dry ingredients until it resembles yellow corn meal mixed with bean sized bits of butter. (If the flour/butter mixture gets warm, refrigerate it for 10 minutes before proceeding.) Add the egg and stir the dough together with a fork or by hand in the bowl. If the dough is dry, sprinkle up to a tablespoon more of cold water over the mixture.

Step 1: Or make the dough in a food processor. With the machine fitted with the metal blade, pulse the flour, sugar, and salt until combined. Add the butter and pulse until it resembles yellow corn meal mixed with bean size bits of butter, about 10 times. Add the egg and pulse 1 to 2 times; don't let the dough form into a ball in the machine. (If the dough is very dry add up to a tablespoon more of cold water.) Remove the bowl from the machine, remove the blade, and bring the dough together by hand.

Step 2: Form the dough into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate until thoroughly chilled, at least 1 hour.

Step 3: Make the filling. Put the lemon juice in a medium bowl. Peel, halve, and core the apples. Cut each half into 4 wedges. Toss the apple with the lemon juice. Add the sugar and toss to combine evenly.

Step 4: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium-high heat. Add the apples, and cook, stirring, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture begins to simmer, about 2 minutes. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook until the apples soften and release most of their juices, about 7 minutes.

Step 5: Strain the apples in a colander over a medium bowl to catch all the juice. Shake the colander to get as much liquid as possible. Return the juices to the skillet, and simmer over medium heat until thickened and lightly caramelized, about 10 minutes.

Step 6: In a medium bowl, toss the apples with the reduced juice and spices. Set aside to cool completely. (This filling can be made up to 2 days ahead and refrigerated or frozen for up to 6 months.)

Stpe 7: Cut the dough in half. On a lightly floured surface, roll each half of dough into a disc about 11 to 12 inches wide. Layer the dough between pieces of parchment or wax paper on a baking sheet, and refrigerate for at least 10 minutes.

Step 8: Place a rack in the lower third of the oven and preheat the oven to 375 degrees F.

Step 9: Line the bottom of a 9-inch pie pan with one of the discs of dough, and trim it so it lays about 1/2 inch beyond the edge of the pan. Put the apple filling in the pan and mound it slightly in the center. Brush the top edges of the dough with the egg. Place the second disc of dough over the top. Fold the top layer of dough under the edge of the bottom layer and press the edges together to form a seal. Flute the edge as desired. Brush the surface of the dough with egg and then sprinkle with sugar. Pierce the top of the dough in several places to allow steam to escape while baking. Refrigerate for at least 15 minutes.

Step 10: Bake the pie on a baking sheet until the crust is golden, about 50 minutes. Cool on a rack before serving. The pie keeps well at room temperature (covered) for 24 hours, or refrigerated for up to 4 days.

Tiramisu

  • Time: 3 hr 30 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Egg Yolks 6
White Sugar 1 cup, divided
Mascarpone Cheese 1 pound
Egg Whites 6, stiffly beaten
Heavy Cream 1/4 cup
Strong Brewed Coffee, cold 1 1/4 cups
Ladyfingers 25
Unsweetened Cocoa powder 1 tablespoon

Step 1: In a medium bowl beat together the egg yolks and 1/3 cup of sugar. Using a wooden spoon stir in mascarpone cheese, beaten egg whites, cream and kirschwasser; stir until smooth. Set aside.

Step 2:Dissolve remaining 2/3 cup sugar in coffee. Quickly, to avoid complete saturation, dip ends of ladyfingers in coffee mixture. Place ladyfingers in a single layer in a 9 x 13 inch glass baking dish. Spread a layer of cheese mixture over the ladyfingers; repeat layers, ending with cheese mixture.

Step 3: Cover and refrigerate for several hours. Sprinkle with cocoa just before serving.

Cinnamon Rolls

  • Time: 3 hr 10 min
  • Serves: 12
Ingredients Quantity Part
Milk, warm to touch 2 cups Dough
Sugar 1/2 cup Dough
Unsalted Butter, melted 8 tablespoons Dough
Active Dry Yeast 2 1/3 teaspoons Dough
All-Purpose Flour, divided 5 cups Dough
Baking Powder 1 teaspoon Dough
Kosher Salt 2 teaspoons Dough
Light Brown Sugar 3/4 cup Filling
Ground Cinnamon 2 tablespoons Filling
Unsalted Butter 12 tablespoons Filling
Cream Cheese, sofened, cubed 4 oz Frosting
Unsalted Butter, melted 2 tablespoons Frosting
Milk 4 tablespoons Frosting
Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon Frosting
Powdered Sugar 1 cup Frosting

Step 1: Make the dough: In a large bowl, whisk together the warm milk, sugar, and melted butter. The mixture should be just warm, registering between 100-110˚F (37-43˚C). If any warmer, allow to cool slightly.

Step 2: Sprinkle the yeast evenly over the milk mixture, stir, and let sit in a warm place for about 10 minutes until the yeast has bloomed.

Step 3: Add 4 cups (500 g) of flour to the milk mixture and stir with a wooden spoon until just combined.

Step 4: Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, until nearly doubled in size.

Step 5: Generously butter 2 9-inch (23 cm) round baking pans and set aside.

Step 6: Make the filling: In a medium bowl, combine the brown sugar, butter, and cinnamon. Mix well, then set aside.

Step 7: Remove the plastic wrap from the dough and add the remaining cup of flour, baking powder, and salt. Stir well, then turn out onto a clean surface. Leave a bit of flour nearby to use as needed, but try not to incorporate too much.

Stpe 8: Knead the dough for at least 10 minutes, adding more flour as necessary, until the dough just loses its stickiness and does not stick to the surface or your hands. The dough should be very smooth and spring back when poked.

Step 9: Roll the dough out into a large rectangle, about ½-inch (1 cm) thick. Fix the corners with a bench scraper or a spatula to make sure they are sharp and even.

Step 10: Spread the filling evenly over the dough.

Step 11:Starting from one short end, roll up the dough into a log and pinch the seam closed. Place seam-side down. Trim any uneven ends.

Step 12:Using unflavored dental floss, cut the log into evenly pieces, about 1½ inches (8cm) thick. Place the cinnamon rolls in the prepared pans, 1 in the center and about 5 around the sides. Cover with a towel and let rise in a warm place for 35-45 minutes, until expanded by about half of their original volume. If you’re saving the cinnamon rolls for later, cover with plastic wrap and freeze for up 3 months.

Step 13:Preheat the oven to 350˚F (180˚C).

Step 14:Bake the cinnamon rolls for 25-30 minutes, until golden brown.

Step 15:While the cinnamon rolls are baking, make the frosting: In a medium bowl, whisk together the cream cheese, melted butter, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and whisk until homogenous and runny.

Step 16:When the cinnamon rolls are finished, let cool for about 10 minutes.

Step 16:Drizzle the frosting over the cinnamon rolls, using the back of a spoon to spread if desired.
